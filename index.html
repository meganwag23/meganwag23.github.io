Background

How I got here

My path into operations was built through doing. Early on, I found myself drawn to the connective tissue of organizations: the systems, the communications, and the behind-the-scenes coordination that makes everything else possible. Over time, I moved from supporting individual projects to owning the operational infrastructure of an entire firm.

At Jefferson & Associates, I joined a fast-scaling public affairs and regulatory strategy firm at a pivotal moment, when startup energy needed to evolve into something sustainable. I helped lead that transition by designing SOPs, building organizational systems from scratch, managing executive communications, and coordinating across clients, attorneys, regulators, and trade associations in the complex, rapidly shifting landscape of cannabis and hemp policy.

What I've learned is that good operations aren't just about efficiency. They're about creating the conditions for people to do their best work. That means writing clearly, thinking structurally, and knowing when to build a system and when to just solve the problem.

Now I'm looking to take that foundation somewhere new. One direction I'm especially drawn to is educational lobbying and policy advocacy. My background sits at the exact intersection those roles require: regulatory communications, legislative outreach, stakeholder coordination, and the organizational infrastructure to support sustained advocacy campaigns. I've spent years translating complex policy issues into clear, compelling communications for diverse audiences, and building the systems that keep coalitions aligned and moving. Education policy feels like a natural extension of that work, applied to something with deep and lasting impact.

More broadly, I'm drawn to organizations navigating real complexity, whether that's policy change, rapid growth, or structural transformation. I want to be in rooms where decisions matter, helping leadership operate with more clarity, communicate with more intention, and build infrastructure that actually holds.
