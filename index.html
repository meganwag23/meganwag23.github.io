How I got here

I started my career in education and spent seven years teaching elementary school. Looking back, what I loved most wasn't just teaching the lessons. It was building a classroom where kids felt safe, supported, and capable of more than they thought they were.

I was the teacher who volunteered for extra responsibilities, joined committees, and looked for ways to improve things when they weren't working. I cared deeply about my students, but I also cared about the systems around them. I wanted to understand why things worked, why they didn't, and what could be done to make them better. My classroom became known for strong academic outcomes, but more importantly, it was a place where students grew in confidence, independence, and resilience.

When I transitioned into operations and public affairs, I was surprised by how much of that same mindset carried over.

At Jefferson & Associates, I joined a growing consulting firm during a period of significant change. As the company expanded, there was an opportunity to create more structure, build stronger internal systems, and establish processes that would support long-term growth. I've helped develop SOPs, manage executive communications, coordinate projects across multiple stakeholders, and keep complex initiatives moving forward. Much of my work sits at the intersection of operations, communications, and relationship management, whether that's working with clients, attorneys, regulators, trade associations, or internal leadership.

Across every role I've held, the common thread has been people. I genuinely enjoy helping others do their best work. Sometimes that means building a process that makes everyone's job easier. Sometimes it means solving a problem that's been sitting on the back burner for too long. Sometimes it simply means being the person who keeps things organized, moving, and connected.

As I think about what comes next, I'm especially interested in opportunities where I can bring together my background in education, operations, communications, and public affairs.

Educational policy and advocacy is one path that feels particularly meaningful to me. I understand education from the perspective of someone who spent years in the classroom, and I also understand how organizations, policymakers, and stakeholders work together to create change. The idea of helping bridge those worlds is exciting because I know firsthand how much good policy matters, and how different things can look on paper versus in practice.

More broadly, I'm drawn to organizations that are growing, evolving, or tackling complicated challenges. I enjoy bringing order to complexity, helping teams communicate more effectively, and creating systems that allow good people to succeed. I don't need to be the loudest person in the room, but I do want to be someone who helps move important work forward.
